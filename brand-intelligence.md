## Identity asset and use

- Local path: `assets/brand/universal-orlando-logo.png`
- The mark is used unmodified for nominative identification. The official first-party navigation asset is content-negotiated as AVIF in the available retrieval environment, so the local file is a public 2023 PNG copy of the same Universal Orlando Resort identity.
- It appears above the fold and on every document surface with an explicit independent-candidate qualifier.
- It is not animated, recolored, traced, merged into a candidate mark or used to imply endorsement.

- `#050814` and `#0c1733`: source-observed night / cinematic digital surfaces and source-sampled logo shadows.
- `#203160` and `#3158a0`: source-sampled from the blue globe.
- `#d7dfea`: source-sampled silver highlight.
- `#a97b16`: source-sampled gold letter treatment.
- `#56d7ff`, `#ffc857`, `#ff745c`: candidate-original operating states for healthy signal, review and recovery. They remain subordinate to the Universal recognition layer.

## Typography evidence and decision

The official destination experience uses bold, compact sans-serif display typography, uppercase navigation labels and high-contrast cinematic hierarchy. No public brand guide or reusable official webfont license was located. The campaign uses installed open fonts Cabin for display and Clear Sans / Arimo for body copy. These are documented structural substitutes; they are not described as official Universal fonts. No proprietary font files are committed.

## Photography, imagery and visual grammar

The official experience uses cinematic dark surfaces, immersive destination photography, bold promotional type, blue and gold celestial cues, portal imagery and high-energy motion. The campaign does not reuse character, attraction or guest photography. Instead it creates an original technical-theatre visual system: scenic flats, stage light, cue language and production books. This keeps employer recognition while avoiding IP-heavy imagery and corporate-site imitation.

## Role translation

- Visible identity: official Universal Orlando Resort logo plus `Candidate vision ... by Russell Dudek`.
- Signature scene: four scenic flats align or shift to show how Scene, Cue, Run and Review protect experience continuity.
- Motion: stage-light sweep, scenic-flat alignment and scenario-driven continuity restoration.
- Interaction: keyboard-accessible operating scenarios change the alignment and production disposition.
- Documents: restrained blue, silver and gold rules with one shared candidate header.

## Independent-candidate distinction

Every route identifies the work as independent candidate material. The footer states that the work is not an official Universal Orlando Resort publication and is not endorsed by Universal.
